# Executors

This section explains some of the most common executor concepts and functions. The section called [Advanced Executor Functionality](Advanced%20Executor%20Functionality.md) explains the rest.

Executors are handles used to control and execute stored content.

The most obvious use for an executor is to assign a sequence with cues for playback. However executors may also be used as handles for other objects, for example a physical key for a specific macro or a speedfader for a group of chasers.

Executors are arranged in Executor Pages. This enables you to easy get a new set of executors merely by changing page.

**Hint:**  
Changing page will not load new content into the physical executors, like on some other consoles. Changing page just instructs the console to **display** another set of executors on your physical hardware. This enables you to have executors from multiple pages active and running at the same time.

Read more about this in the [Executor Pages topic](../Pages/Advanced%20Executor%20Functionality/Executor%20Pages.md) (Advanced Executor Functionality section).

A more detailed explanation of the executors are found in the [What are Executors topic](../Pages/Executors/What%20are%20executors.md).

Executors are physical keys and faders, but they can also be represented as virtual executors on the screens - [Executors on the Screens](../Pages/Executors/Executors%20on%20the%20screens.md).

There are two ways to see all the active executors, this also provides a fast way to turn Off executors - read about it in the [Looking at the Active Executors topic](../Pages/Executors/Looking%20at%20the%20active%20executors.md).

Assigning an object to an executor and changing the function on the faders and keys are explained in the [Assign a Function topic](../Pages/Executors/Assign%20a%20function.md).

Finally some of the common options that are not already covered in this section and in the [Cues and Sequences section](Cues%20and%20Sequences.md), are explained in the [Common Executor Options topic](../Pages/Executors/Common%20executor%20options.md).
